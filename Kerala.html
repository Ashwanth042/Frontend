About Kerala

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is known for its lush greenery, tranquil backwaters, rich cultural heritage, and high literacy rate. Often referred to as "God's Own Country," Kerala boasts a unique blend of natural beauty and vibrant traditions. The state is famous for its network of interconnected rivers, lagoons, and canals that form the scenic backwaters, drawing tourists from around the world. It is also known for its classical arts, Ayurveda treatments, and delicious cuisine, which includes coconut-rich curries and seafood. Kerala's population enjoys one of the highest human development indices in India, with strong emphasis on education and healthcare. With a tropical climate, diverse wildlife, and picturesque hill stations like Munnar and Wayanad, Kerala is a popular destination for nature lovers and cultural explorers alike.

Green Attractions

Athirappilly Water Falls

Athirappilly Falls, located in Kerala's Thrissur district, is the largest waterfall in the state. Known as the "Niagara of India," it drops from 80 feet amidst lush greenery, attracting nature lovers and tourists alike.

Thattekkad Bird Sanctuary

Thattekkad Bird Sanctuary, spread along the banks of the Periyar River in Kerala, is India's first designated bird sanctuary. It's a haven for birdwatchers, with over 300 species—both resident and migratory—thriving amid dense evergreen forests and tranquil waterways.

Thenmala Eco‑Tourism

Thenmala Eco‑Tourism, nestled in Kerala's Western Ghats, is India’s first planned eco-tourism destination—offering serene forest trails, bamboo rafting on Neyyar Lake, and a canopy walkway amidst lush greenery.

Silent Valley National Park

Silent Valley National Park, located in Kerala’s Nilgiri Hills, is a pristine tropical rainforest known for its rich biodiversity and rare species like the lion-tailed macaque. It's one of the most ecologically significant areas in India.

Marari Beach

Marari Beach, located in Mararikulam village in Kerala's Alappuzha district, is a tranquil coastal retreat known for its serene ambiance and natural beauty. Often referred to as one of the world's top hammock beaches, it offers a peaceful alternative to Kerala's more commercialized shores

Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple, located in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, depicted in a reclining posture on the serpent Anantha. Known for its stunning Dravidian architecture and immense wealth, it is one of the 108 Divya Desams and a major spiritual and cultural landmark in India.
